Active Research

Current Projects

Ongoing research initiatives spanning epidemiology, health systems, and digital health innovation.

Research Portfolio

My Current Projects

Each project addresses a critical gap in public health knowledge with robust methodology and multi-stakeholder partnerships.

01

AI-Powered Disease Surveillance System for Low-Resource Settings

● Active

This project develops and validates a machine learning-driven disease surveillance platform designed for deployment in health facilities with limited digital infrastructure. The system integrates real-time data from community health workers, facility-level reports, and environmental sensors to enable early outbreak detection with a minimum 5-day advance warning.

📅 Duration: 2023 – 2026
💰 Funder: Wellcome Trust / NIH
🌍 Sites: 3 countries
📄 Project Page 📊 Data Dashboard 📖 Publications
02

Non-Communicable Disease Prevention Through Community Engagement

● Ongoing

A community-based participatory research project evaluating the effectiveness of culturally-adapted health promotion interventions on NCD risk factors, including hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. The project uses a cluster-randomised controlled trial design across 24 communities with a 3-year follow-up period.

📅 Duration: 2022 – 2025
💰 Funder: WHO / National Research Fund
👥 Participants: 4,800
📄 Protocol Paper 🗺️ Trial Registry
03

Electronic Health Records Implementation & Quality Improvement

● Ongoing

This implementation science project evaluates barriers, facilitators, and health outcomes associated with EHR adoption in public district hospitals. Using a mixed-methods approach, the research develops a practical implementation framework and toolkit for national scale-up of digital health records with a focus on interoperability and data quality.

📅 Duration: 2024 – 2027
💰 Funder: Digital Health Initiative
🏥 Sites: 18 hospitals
📄 Study Overview 🔧 Open Toolkit
04

Antimicrobial Resistance Epidemiology in Community Settings

⚙ Planning Phase

A prospective cohort study investigating the emergence, spread, and determinants of antimicrobial resistance in community settings. The project leverages genomic surveillance, spatial epidemiology, and social network analysis to map transmission pathways and identify high-risk population clusters for targeted intervention.

📅 Duration: 2025 – 2029
💰 Funder: Under review – UKRI
🔬 Approach: Genomic + Spatial
